Celia Howes

Board Candidate Statement
District 5

David McDonald leaves big shoes to fill in the District 5 Board position. His enthusiasm for criminal defense, OCDLA and its functions draws a clear road map for those of us who want to make a positive contribution through board membership. My feet are smaller, but I am looking forward to following up on David’s efforts and bringing my voice and energy to the OCDLA Board.

Stepping outside of my practice to contribute to our defense community has been an important aspect of my career.  I have enjoyed serving on the OCDLA Education Committee, presenting at seminars and working informally with lobbyists and legislators on OCDLA’s efforts to impact relevant legislation.  I feel that now is the time to take on a more integral role in the continuing growth and development of our organization by serving as your representative on our Board.

As a board member, I hope to expand my contribution to our community. I aim to ensure a continued focus on OCDLA’s time-tested functions –  to keep our membership on the cutting edge with continuing education seminars and the Library of Defense and to make our collective voice heard on targeted legislative action in Salem. I will also keep the board exploring new ideas and fresh topics, as OCDLA benefits from the innovation and creativity of its membership.  We are one of the, if not the most, supportive criminal defense organizations in the nation, and our future gets brighter everyday as new zealous defenders become a part of our community. I want to be in a place to help steer us into the future, and I ask that you support me for the Board.
