Russell Barnett

Board Candidate Statement
District 5

I am asking for the privilege to serve another two years on the OCDLA Board of Directors. In the past two years on the Board, I have been an active participant in all Board meetings and functions. I have held the office of Secretary in my second year on the Board, and I want to continue to serve the organization and its members.

When preparing this statement, I started to list my accomplishments and detail my involvement with OCDLA and the Board of Directors, but as I did so, I realized that chronicling my achievements is the antithesis of being a member of the Board of Directors. The Board is a collective of individuals exercising stewardship over the organization known as OCDLA. Key to that stewardship is Board members working together to address concerns, test ideas and question issues before making formal commitments. The Board addresses issues from pricing the laminate “Quick Guides” to purchasing a building for the organization.

All of these decisions are made with careful and thoughtful discussion. Board discussions are dynamic and rely on the relationships and trust that develops among and between the members. I have worked very hard to develop meaningful relationships between my fellow board members, and I have earned their trust, as they have earned mine.

In addition to my experience working with the individuals currently on the Board of Directors, I ask that you consider the nature of my practice. I am exclusively an Oregon lawyer. Through my firm, Squires & Lopez, I specialize in criminal defense. Although I am a member of the state and federal bars of Oregon, I devote the bulk of my practice to indigent criminal defense in the state court system. I am committed to securing adequate funding for court-appointed case work, as well as overall funding for the judicial branch. As an organization, we have no other issue more important to our members than maintaining funding for indigent defense. I cannot say that I alone can or will effect legislative funding decisions, but I can say that it is the issue central to my livelihood, and I will not let it fall by the wayside.

In the next two years, I believe OCDLA needs to take the lead in the establishment of statutory requirements for preservation of DNA evidence in criminal cases, as well as the formulation of legal avenues to use that evidence to challenge convictions. The current state of the law in Oregon, as in many states, is deplorable. I would like the opportunity, as a Board member, to help OCDLA change Oregon’s laws regarding preservation and utilization of DNA evidence.

I ask for your support to give me the privilege of serving a second term on your Board, and for the opportunity to complete my goals (building purchase and holding Board office), and to continue to provide stewardship for our organization. I appreciate your trust in me. I will not let you down. Thank you, and please contact me for any questions or concerns you may have.
