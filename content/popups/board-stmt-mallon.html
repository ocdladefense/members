Gordon Mallon

Board Candidate Statement
District 6

As I was growing up, my mother taught me that it is each person’s responsibility to make his or her community a better place by volunteering service to the community. After talking to various current board members, I realized that this organization that I love, OCDLA, is at a crossroads. I feel a responsibility to step up once again and offer my experience and energy to help however I can to guide this organization to a bright future. During my prior four years on the board and my year as president, I helped bring about better funding for indigent defense than we had previously seen. I worked with Ross Shepard to bring the issue before the OSB house of delegates, and was successful in making the indigent defense budget a high priority of the OSB lobbyists. I also helped bring about outreach efforts from OCDLA to our members in the far flung corners of our state. I believe that we as a board were in better touch with our membership than we had ever been up to that point. Now we face some new challenges. Among them are:

• Continuing the fight for stable, adequate funding for indigent defense.

• Finding an office for OCDLA that we own. This makes excellent financial sense.

• Making OCDLA even more valuable to our members who practice juvenile law.

• Planning for that long dreaded day that our Executive Director, John Potter,  decides that his time of service to us all is over. We will never be able to replace John, but we are going to have to do something.

I have counted my past service to this organization as some of the most valuable time I have spent, and thank each of you for your past, current and future support.
