Tahra Sinks

Board Candidate Statement
District 6

We know change is coming. We don’t know if it will be for the better.
The looming budget shortfall in Oregon leaves the state facing an array of difficult choices. And while most of those options will hurt, it is incumbent on us to ensure that Oregon emerges from this crisis stronger – and with saner public safety policy.

This space is reserved for my candidate statement. I can sum that up quickly: Please vote for me.
I have been honored to serve the past two years on the OCDLA Board of Directors, and I am seeking election to a second term. I have been proud to offer the perspective of a juvenile law attorney on the Board, to help with conferences and outreach, and to speak on behalf of OCDLA at the Capitol.

Your all-volunteer Board is collaborative and is working for you, and we are committed to helping the OCDLA navigate these turbulent economic and political times.

But I also cannot sugarcoat our situation: We face a daunting challenge.

The state’s budget chasm will demand more than nibbling at the edges of reform. Oregon will need to reinvent itself and reexamine what is truly a “core service.”

Rahm Emanuel, chief of staff for President Barack Obama, famously said that you should “never let a crisis go to waste.”

We should not.

Now is the time to ask whether Oregon can afford to stay the course on Measure 11 and to incarcerate more people every year, including the mentally ill – or should we instead prioritize education and treatment programs that are proven to actually make communities safer.

Now is the time to ask if we want more crime and more broken families – or should we instead invest in early prevention efforts and better-trained caseworkers.

And while pressure will be intense to spend less representing the accused, now is the time to ask whether Oregon can underpay – and still expect justice. 

Those choices will affect our practices and our livelihoods.

But it is also bigger than us.

We are talking about the future of our state, which will affect not just our own families, but every family. There will be winners and losers in these austere times.

Oregon should invest in smarter public safety policy…which will mean more dollars for education, human services and natural resources – and ultimately will lead to a better quality of life.

Change is coming, that much is certain. We don’t know which path leaders will choose. Amid the cacophony of conflicting views at the Capitol, it is our responsibility to ensure legislators understand which options will lead Oregon to a better future.

We don’t just need a voice, we need a megaphone.

Today, more than ever, we need the OCDLA.

With the guidance of the Board, OCDLA needs to continue providing its signature training programs, and to become an even stronger, nimbler and vigilant political engine.

Our future – and Oregon’s future – demands no less.

Please support my candidacy for the OCDLA Board of Directors.
