2015 Juvenile Training Immersion Program

April 16–17, 2015
Hallmark Resort, Newport, OR

OCDLA is proud to offer a unique limited enrollment training opportunity for Oregon juvenile delinquency practitioners — a Juvenile Training Immersion Program (JTIP). Developed by the National Juvenile Defender Center, JTIP is meant to be the “gold standard” in training for juvenile defenders and is intended to help defenders across the country offer the highest level of advocacy for their clients. 

The NJDC program consists of 40 training modules or lessons. OCDLA will present two of those modules:
• Competency to Stand Trial (Lesson #15)
• Challenging Juvenile Sex Cases (Lesson #32)

Enrollment limited to 27 juvenile delinquency practitioners (one from each judicial district).

To be considered for this training, contact your supervisor or contract administrator.

Deadline to register is March 2.

Other questions? Call 541-686-8716, or email Tracye.

Requirements
• Public Defense Contractors select one attorney from their judicial district to attend the JTIP training ($100) and the Juvenile Law Seminar ($255). 
• Attorney must be an OCDLA member who is involved in juvenile delinquency public defense representation.
• Attorney must also register for OCDLA’s April 17–18 Juvenile Law Seminar.
• Attorney must share information from the training at brown bag style CLE with others in their district upon completion of the training.

Deadline
The deadline to select an attorney and notify OCDLA with payment is March 2.

Scholarships
Scholarship assistance can be requested from OCDLA.

Cancellations
This is a commitment. It will be difficult to find replacements for those who cancel. Because of this and the reduced fee, there are no refunds for canceling JTIP.
